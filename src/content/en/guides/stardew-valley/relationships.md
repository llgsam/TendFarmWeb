## Why Relationships Matter

Friendships in Stardew Valley aren't just flavor. Each heart level unlocks something: a recipe, a useful item, or a story moment that deepens your understanding of the town. Higher friendship means villagers send you gifts in the mail. Marriage brings a companion who helps on the farm and gives you daily food buffs.

More practically: Pelican Town is small. You'll walk past these people every day. Getting to know them transforms the town from background scenery into a community you care about.

---

## How the System Works

### Friendship Points and Hearts

Every villager tracks friendship in points. **250 points = 1 heart**. Hearts cap at **10 for regular villagers**, **14 for spouses** (8 to become dateable, then 14 after marriage with a Mermaid's Pendant).

**Ways to gain points:**
- Talk to them: +20 per day (only first conversation counts)
- Give a liked gift: +45 points
- Give a loved gift: +80 points
- Give on their birthday: ×8 multiplier (so a loved gift on a birthday = 640 points)
- Complete a bundle item they mentioned
- Help with a request on the bulletin board: varies

**Ways to lose points:**
- Give a hated gift: -40 points
- Miss a birthday (you don't give a gift): -20 points × number of days missed
- Marry without first completing a heart event: impossible (the game blocks it)

### The 2-Gift-Per-Week Rule

You can only give each villager **2 gifts per week** (the week resets every Monday). Exception: on their birthday, you can give 1 gift that doesn't count toward the weekly limit.

This means friendship building is a slow, sustained effort over seasons — not something you can rush in one day.

## Marriage: How It Works

### Requirements

To marry a villager in Stardew Valley:

1. **Reach 8 hearts** with them (requires completing all heart events up to 8)
2. **See all heart events** (they trigger automatically when you enter the right location at the right time — sometimes you need to trigger them in specific seasons)
3. **Upgrade your house** to have a bedroom (Robin's construction, 50,000g + 150 Wood)
4. **Buy a Mermaid's Pendant** from the Old Mariner (appears on the beach on rainy days in Spring and Summer) for 5,000g

Once you give the Mermaid's Pendant, you're engaged. Marriage happens 3 days later.

### What Spouses Do

After marriage, your spouse:
- Moves into your farmhouse
- Has a dedicated room on the right side
- Does small chores: sometimes waters crops, sometimes feeds animals
- Gives you gifts and cooked food
- Has unique daily dialogue that changes by season and weather

Friendship continues after marriage — hearts can rise to 14. Give your spouse a gift weekly and talk to them daily to maintain high friendship. Neglected spouses become slightly less helpful.

## Friendship Benefits Beyond Marriage

Even if you're not pursuing marriage, high friendship with every villager provides:

**Recipes**: Most villagers send you cooking recipes by mail at higher heart levels. These recipes are the only way to learn many dishes.

**Gifts by mail**: Villagers occasionally send items — seeds, food, ores — at 3+ hearts.

**Sam at 3 hearts**: He gives you a recipe for Pizza.

**Willy at 4 hearts**: Gives you a recipe for Dish O' The Sea, and later unlocks boat access.

**Wizard at 4 hearts**: Unlocks the ability to change your character's appearance.

**Dwarf at 4 hearts**: Requires learning Dwarvish (donate 4 Dwarvish Scrolls to museum). Can then buy rare items from him.

**Krobus (Void creature)**: Can become a roommate instead of a spouse — unique option if you don't want to pursue romance.

## Year 1 Social Strategy

You can't max all relationships in Year 1 — there's not enough time and gift capacity. Prioritize:

**Talk to everyone daily** — it's free and takes 30 seconds of playtime to hit 5-6 villagers on your morning walk to the mine.

**Prioritize the marriage candidate you're interested in** — focus your 2 weekly gifts on them to reach 8 hearts by end of Year 1 or early Year 2.

**Never miss birthdays** — check the calendar each week. A single birthday gift can be worth a week of normal gifting.

**Give cheap loved gifts** — Amethyst (found in mines) is loved by Abigail and Emily. Leek (foraged in Spring) is liked by many. Daffodil (Spring forage) is liked by most. These are free and efficient.
